Bonus Depreciation Note
The One Big Beautiful Bill Act permanently restored 100% bonus depreciation for property acquired and placed in service after January 19, 2025. Earlier years follow the original phase down for properties acquired before January 19, 2025: 60% in 2024, 40% in 2025, 20% in 2026.
Land Allocation

What is the property's land allocation?

Land is not depreciable. Only the building portion of your basis qualifies for cost segregation and bonus depreciation, so this number directly drives your year one savings.

% land
Leave blank to use the 20 percent national average. If you have your county assessor's allocation or an appraisal, enter it here.
Why this matters
Properties on the water, on large land plots, near the West Coast, or in highly desirable areas tend to have higher land allocations. The wrong allocation in either direction can swing year one savings by tens of thousands of dollars. For an actual filing, verify with your advisor using the county assessor's land-to-improvement ratio, a recent appraisal, or a cost segregation engineer's allocation, whichever is most defensible for your facts and circumstances.

Your Hours

How many hours per year will you spend on the property?

Includes guest communications, cleaning coordination, maintenance, listings, bookkeeping. Also include furnishing, interior design, and initial setup. This number is a main driver of the strategy.

Others' Hours

Of all other people working on the property, how much time does the next highest person spend on the property?

Property manager, cleaner, general contractor, co-host. Count whichever individual logs the most hours. Not the sum of everyone else's hours, just the highest individual.

If you're not sure, an estimate is fine.

Personal Use

Personal use days planned in year one?

Even one personal day forces a proportional allocation of expenses (including depreciation) to nondeductible personal use. This hurts most in the year of acquisition.

What Counts as Personal Use
Time spent working on the property is not personal use. If the property is out of town and you stay overnight, log more than 4 hours of work that day to make it a work day instead of a personal day. Personal use exceeding the greater of 14 days or 10% of total rental days could trigger reclassification as a secondary residence under §280A.
Operating Loss & Furnishings

Estimated operating loss and furnishing costs?

Depreciation is the biggest piece of year-one loss, but not the only piece. Operating expenses and furnishings stack on top.

$
Mortgage interest (not principal), property tax, insurance, utilities, cleaning, fees, net of rental income.
$
One-time cost to furnish the property. Eligible for 100% bonus depreciation.
Why we ask
A typical first-year STR operates at a loss due to the cost to set up the property. These additional out-of-pocket expenses add to your bonus depreciation to determine your total non-passive rental loss for the year.
Exclude Major Renovations
Do not include major renovation or repair costs in either field. If you're planning a significant renovation, the analysis will be more complex. Consult your CPA to model the full impact.
Cost Segregation

Cost segregation study?

Cost seg is what unlocks year one savings. Without it, depreciation is taken straight-line over 39 years.

Note — Return Already Filed
If you've already filed your return without a cost segregation study, you'll need to either amend the return or file Form 3115 (Application for Change in Accounting Method) in a future year to capture the missed depreciation. We'll model the year-one savings here, but talk to your CPA about which path makes sense for your situation.
